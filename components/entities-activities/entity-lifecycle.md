# Entity lifecycle flow

## generatedAtTime {=prov:generatedAtTime .owl:DatatypeProperty label}

> The time at which an entity was completely created and is available for use. {comment @en}

Sets [dateTime] {+xsd:dateTime ?range} literal for an [entity] {+prov:Entity ?domain}.

May be qualified by [atTime] {+prov:atTime ?prov:qualifiedForm} in a [Generation] {+prov:Generation ?prov:qualifiedForm} attached to the entity.

> It is the intent that the property chain holds: (prov:qualifiedGeneration o prov:atTime) rdfs:subPropertyOf prov:generatedAtTime. {prov:editorialNote @en}

## wasGeneratedBy {=prov:wasGeneratedBy .owl:ObjectProperty label}

Records that one [entity] {+prov:Entity ?domain} was generated by an [activity] {+prov:Activity ?range}.

May be qualified by [qualifiedGeneration] {+prov:qualifiedGeneration ?prov:qualifiedForm} with [Generation] {+prov:Generation ?prov:qualifiedForm} attached.

Sub-property: [wasInfluencedBy] {+prov:wasInfluencedBy ?subPropertyOf}

Inverse: [generated] {prov:inverse}

## generated {=prov:generated .owl:ObjectProperty}

Connects [activities] {+prov:Activity ?domain} to [entities] {+prov:Entity ?range} with a [generation] {+prov:Generation ?prov:sharedDefinitionWith} relation as a sub-property of [influenced] {+prov:influenced ?subPropertyOf}.

Inverse: [wasGeneratedBy] {prov:inverse} - one of few defined inverse properties - it's the inverse of [wasGeneratedBy] {+prov:wasGeneratedBy ?owl:inverseOf}.

> prov:generated is one of few inverse property defined, to allow Activity-oriented assertions in addition to Entity-oriented assertions. {comment @en}


## qualifiedGeneration {=prov:qualifiedGeneration owl:ObjectProperty label}

> If this Activity prov:generated Entity :e, then it can qualify how it performed the Generation using prov:qualifiedGeneration [ a prov:Generation;  prov:entity :e; :foo :bar ]. {comment @en}

Informs that an [entity] {+prov:Entity ?domain} was [generated] {+prov:Generation ?range ?prov:sharesDefinitionWith} by an [activity] {+prov:Activity ?domain}.

A qualified version of [wasGeneratedBy] {+prov:wasGeneratedBy ?prov:unqualifiedForm}.

Sub-property: [qualifiedInfluence] {+prov:qualifiedInfluence ?subPropertyOf}

Inverse: [qualifiedGenerationOf] {prov:inverse}


## Generation {=prov:Generation .Class label}

> Generation is the completion of production of a new entity by an activity. This entity did not exist before generation and becomes available for usage after this generation. {prov:definition @en}

Sub-class of [ActivityInfluence] {+prov:ActivityInfluence ?subClassOf} and [InstantaneousEvent] {+prov:InstantaneousEvent ?subClassOf}.

> An instance of prov:Generation provides additional descriptions about the binary prov:wasGeneratedBy relation from a generated prov:Entity to the prov:Activity that generated it. For example, :cake prov:wasGeneratedBy :baking; prov:qualifiedGeneration [ a prov:Generation; prov:activity :baking; :foo :bar ]. {comment @en}

## invalidatedAtTime {=prov:invalidatedAtTime .owl:DatatypeProperty label}

> The time at which an entity was invalidated (i.e., no longer usable). {comment @en}

Sets [dateTime] {+xsd:dateTime ?range} literal for an [entity] {+prov:Entity ?domain}.

May be qualified by [atTime] {+prov:atTime ?prov:qualifiedForm} in an [Invalidation] {+prov:Invalidation ?prov:qualifiedForm} attached to the entity.

> It is the intent that the property chain holds: (prov:qualifiedInvalidation o prov:atTime) rdfs:subPropertyOf prov:invalidatedAtTime. {prov:editorialNote @en}

## wasInvalidatedBy {=prov:wasInvalidatedBy .owl:ObjectProperty label}

Connects an [entity] {+prov:Entity ?domain} to an [activity] {+prov:Activity ?range} that terminated it.

May be qualified by a [qualifiedInvalidation] {+prov:qualifiedInvalidation ?prov:qualifiedForm} with [Invalidation] {+prov:Invalidation ?prov:qualifiedForm} attached.

Sub-property of [wasInfluencedBy] {+prov:wasInfluencedBy ?subPropertyOf}.

Inverse: [invalidated] {prov:inverse}

## invalidated {=prov:invalidated .owl:ObjectProperty label}

> prov:invalidated is one of few inverse property defined, to allow Activity-oriented assertions in addition to Entity-oriented assertions. {prov:editorialNote @en}

Connects an [Activity] {+prov:Activity ?domain} with an [Entity] {+prov:Entity ?range} that was [invalidated] {+prov:Invalidation ?prov:sharesDefinitionWith} by it.

A sub-property of [influenced] {+prov:influenced ?subPropertyOf}.

Inverse: [wasInvalidatedBy] {prov:inverse}.

## qualifiedInvalidation {=prov:qualifiedInvalidation  owl:ObjectProperty label}

> If this Entity prov:wasInvalidatedBy Activity :a, then it can qualify how it was invalidated using prov:qualifiedInvalidation [ a prov:Invalidation;  prov:activity :a; :foo :bar ]. {comment @en}

Records the [invalidation] {+prov:Invalidation ?range ?prov:sharesDefinitionWith} of an [entity] {+prov:Entity ?domain} by an [activity] {+prov:Activity ?domain}.

A qualified version of [wasInvalidatedBy] {+prov:wasInvalidatedBy ?prov:unqualifiedForm}.

Inverse: [qualifiedInvalidationOf] {prov:inverse}

## Invalidation {=prov:Invalidation .Class label}

Sub-class of [ActivityInfluence] {+prov:ActivityInfluence ?subClassOf} and [InstantaneousEvent] {+prov:InstantaneousEvent ?subClassOf}.

> Invalidation is the start of the destruction, cessation, or expiry of an existing entity by an activity. The entity is no longer available for use (or further invalidation) after invalidation. Any generation or usage of an entity precedes its invalidation. {prov:definition}

> Given that an invalidation is the start of destruction, cessation, or expiry, it is instantaneous. {comment}

Entities have a duration. Generation marks the beginning of an entity, whereas invalidation marks its end.

## Summary

The entity lifecycle flow captures the fundamental story of how entities come into existence, persist through time, and eventually cease to be. This flow provides the temporal backbone of provenance by modeling the complete journey of entities from their birth through their active life to their eventual death.

At its core, this flow addresses a critical question in provenance: "When did this thing exist, and what brought it into or out of existence?" The answer unfolds through a clear pattern of generation and invalidation events, each precisely timed and causally linked to specific activities. Every entity begins with a generation moment - a discrete point in time when an activity completes the creation of something new and makes it available for use. This entity then exists stably until its invalidation moment, when another activity terminates its existence and renders it unavailable.

This temporal framing enables powerful real-world applications. In document management, it tracks when reports are created, published, and eventually deprecated, providing clear audit trails for compliance and governance. For data systems, it monitors freshness windows and expiration schedules, ensuring consumers know exactly when datasets become valid or obsolete. In physical asset tracking, it follows equipment from deployment through maintenance cycles to retirement, supporting lifecycle management and resource planning.

The flow's strength lies in its precise temporal precision. The generatedAtTime and invalidatedAtTime properties establish exact boundaries for entity existence, while the atTime property in qualified forms provides event-level granularity. This temporal accuracy supports sophisticated queries about entity overlap, validity periods, and temporal relationships between different entities.

Crucially, the flow maintains clear activity relationships through bidirectional properties. The wasGeneratedBy and wasInvalidatedBy relations link entities to their responsible activities, while their inverse properties (generated, invalidated) enable activity-centric queries. This dual perspective allows analysts to ask both "What did this activity create?" and "What created this entity?" depending on their investigative needs.

The qualified forms through Generation and Invalidation classes enrich these basic relationships with contextual depth. They capture not just that an entity was generated, but how, where, by whom, and under what circumstances. Similarly, invalidation events can include details about termination reasons, processes, and environmental conditions. This contextual richness transforms simple temporal bookkeeping into meaningful provenance narratives.

By providing complete lifecycle coverage with temporal accuracy and activity integration, this flow becomes the foundation for temporal analysis, compliance reporting, and resource management. It enables organizations to answer critical questions about entity evolution, maintain audit trails for regulatory requirements, and optimize resource usage through precise lifecycle understanding.
