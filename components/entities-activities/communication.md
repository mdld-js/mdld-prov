# Inter-activity communication flow

## wasInformedBy {=prov:wasInformedBy .owl:ObjectProperty label}

> An activity a2 is dependent on or informed by another activity a1, by way of some unspecified entity that is generated by a1 and used by a2. {comment @en}

Connects one [activity] {+prov:Activity ?domain} to another [activity] {+prov:Activity ?range}.

May be qualified by [qualifiedCommunication] {+prov:qualifiedCommunication ?prov:qualifiedForm} with [Communication] {+prov:Communication ?prov:qualifiedForm} attached.

Sub-property of [wasInfluencedBy] {+prov:wasInfluencedBy ?subPropertyOf}.

Inverse: [informed] {prov:inverse}.

## qualifiedCommunication {=prov:qualifiedCommunication .owl:ObjectProperty label}

> If this Activity prov:wasInformedBy Activity :a, then it can qualify how it was influenced using prov:qualifiedCommunication [ a prov:Communication;  prov:activity :a; :foo :bar ]. {comment @en}

Clarifies if an [activity] {+prov:Activity ?domain} was [communicated] {+prov:Communication ?range ?prov:sharesDefinitionWith ?prov:qualifiedForm} by another one.

Sub-property of [qualifiedInfluence] {+prov:qualifiedInfluence ?subPropertyOf}.

Inverse: [qualifiedCommunicationOf] {rov:inverse}.

## Communication {=prov:Communication .Class label}

Sub-class of [ActivityInfluence] {+prov:ActivityInfluence ?subClassOf}.

Qualifies the [wasInformedBy] {+prov:wasInformedBy ?prov:unqualifiedForm}. 

> Communication is the exchange of some unspecified entity by two activities, one activity using some entity generated by the other. {prov:definition}

> A communication implies that activity a2 is dependent on another a1, by way of some unspecified entity that is generated by a1 and used by a2. {comment}

==============

## Summary

The communication flow captures the essential dependency relationships between activities, modeling how processes inform and influence each other through the exchange of entities. This flow answers the fundamental question: "Which activities influenced this activity, and through what communication channels?"

Communication represents the exchange of entities between activities - where one activity generates something that another activity subsequently uses. This creates a dependency chain where the informed activity relies on the informing activity's output, establishing clear causal relationships in complex workflows. The flow provides the backbone for understanding process orchestration, pipeline dependencies, and workflow coordination across diverse domains.

The wasInformedBy relationship connects activities in a directed dependency graph, enabling sophisticated analysis of process chains, bottleneck identification, and impact assessment. The bidirectional properties support flexible querying - both "What influenced this activity?" and "Which activities did this influence?" - while qualified Communication classes enrich these relationships with contextual detail about the nature and circumstances of the communication.

By capturing these inter-activity dependencies, the flow becomes essential for workflow optimization, root cause analysis, and process improvement initiatives. It enables organizations to understand how processes connect, identify critical paths, and maintain comprehensive audit trails of process coordination and information flow.
